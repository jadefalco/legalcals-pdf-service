Landlord Checklist

Before Serving the Notice

  • Confirm the rent was due on the date stated in the tenancy agreement.
  • Calculate the exact amount of rent still owing, including any permitted late fees.
  • Verify the tenant names match the tenancy agreement.
  • Ensure the rental unit address is complete and accurate.

When Completing the Notice

  • Fill in all required fields on Page 1.
  • State the full rent outstanding and the correct due date.
  • Give the tenant the full 10 clear days to pay or dispute.
  • Sign and date the notice before service.

After Serving the Notice

  • Keep a copy of the signed notice for your records.
  • Record the date, time, and method of service.
  • If the tenant pays in full by the deadline, the notice is cancelled.
  • If the tenant does not pay, you may apply for an Order of Possession.

Service Instructions

Permitted Methods of Service

Under the Residential Tenancy Act, a landlord may serve a notice on a tenant using one of the following methods:

  • Personal service on the tenant.
  • Leaving a copy with an adult who appears to live with the tenant.
  • Mailing the notice to the tenant at the rental unit address.
  • Sending the notice by fax, email, or other electronic method if the tenant has agreed in writing to accept service electronically.
  • Leaving a copy in the mailbox or mail slot at the rental unit address.

Calculating the Effective Service Date

When service is by mail, the notice is generally considered served 5 days after mailing. For personal service, electronic service (with consent), or service by leaving a copy at the rental unit, the notice is usually considered served on the same day.

Saturdays, Sundays, and statutory holidays are typically excluded when counting the 10‑day period. Landlords should verify the current RTB rules for calculating time.

Proof of Service

Keep detailed records of how and when the notice was served. If you mail the notice, retain proof of mailing. If you serve electronically, keep a copy of the sent message and any read receipt or delivery confirmation.

Tenant Rights Summary

Right to Dispute

A tenant who receives a 10‑Day Notice for unpaid rent has the right to dispute the notice by applying for dispute resolution with the Residential Tenancy Branch (RTB). The application must be filed within the deadline shown on the notice.

Payment Cancellation Rule

The tenant may cancel the notice by paying the landlord the full amount of rent owing on or before the deadline date stated in the notice. Once the full amount is paid, the landlord cannot continue the eviction process based on this notice.

Important Timelines

  • Effective service date: the date the notice is considered received.
  • 10 clear days: the time the tenant has to pay the rent or dispute.
  • Dispute resolution deadline: within the period set out on the notice.
  • Order of Possession: a landlord may apply only after the notice period expires if rent remains unpaid.
